How I Thanos Snapped My Debt Of $48,315 At The Age Of 26

  • May 11, 2019 BY CHRISTOPHER BURDETTE

I get asked all the time, "how did you pay off all that debt?". I honestly wish I could respond to that question by telling people that I snapped my fingers like Thanos did in the Avengers: Inifinty War movie and poof all my debt went away. However, I can't because life doesn't work that way... unless you hit the lotto. Reality is I set a goal and stuck with it to the end.

My name is Christopher Burdette, and I paid off $48,315 of debt in 30 months at the age of 26. This journey was not easy but itâ€™s possible!!! Here are 3 things that help me pay off my debt.

1. Motivation

Motivation is the key factor to paying off debt and not quiting. My biggest motivation on why I started this debt-free journey was my daughter Aubrey.

In 2017 after my daughter was born, I had a car and student loan. I knew that once I paid off all this debt, I would be able to do 2 things for my daughter. First I would be able to put money aside for my daughter to go to college debt free. Last and most importantly, I would be able to spend more quality time with my daughter.

2. Budgeting

Budgeting is the key factor to being in control of your money. Budgeting is your detailed plan. Budgeting is your friend.

When I first started budgeting using the EveryDollar app , I was horrible at it. It was not until I used the zero-based budgeting technique that I got good at it. Doing this technique, I realized that I had extra income that I could throw at my debt. In addition to that, I gained control of my money and I haven't looked back since.

3. Scarfice

Scarficing is delaying gratification. It is what determines how bad you want to acheive a goal. During this debt-free journey, I scarficed a lot but here are the top 2.

First, I got a roommate. I'm big on having privacy; however, I was willing to give that up in order to save money. My roommate and I together pay around $1000.00 a month for rent, so separately that's $500.00.

Last, I stop eating out. I love food, and there wasn't a day I didn't think about going to Chick-fil-A and ordering a number 1. However, I had to constantly remind myself that I could get way more food at a Walmart. This scarfice was hard, but in the end it created some good habits. I meal prep almost every week and work out about 3 times a week.

That's It!

This journey wasn't easy, but because I found my motivation, started budgeting and scarfice some things it was possible.

I hope this blog post is insipring to those who want to take this journey. Let me know if you have any questions!
