How to add an untrusted ios shortcut

Are you seeing this message?

While trying to add a shortcut from a downloaded link, this error has probably appeared.


How do I fix this error?

To fix this error, you need to allow untrusted ios shortcuts.

Let me show you how to do that. Click next.

1. Navigate to your ios shortcut settings

A) Go to your settings and scroll down to your ios shortcut app. Click it.

B) After clicking it, you should see a toggle to allow untrusted shortcuts.

C) Enable the toggle button.


But what if that toggle button is greyed out...

Don't worry that just means you haven't ran any shortcuts yet.

So click on next, and I'll show you how to run a sample shortcut to enable the toggle button.

2. Run a sample shortcut to enable greyed out toggle button

A) Go to your shortcuts app, click on my shortcuts at the bottom.

B) Scroll down to the bottom and look for the "What's a shortcut?" widget. Click on it.

C) Run the shortcut by pressing the play icon on the bottom right.

D) Go back to settings > shortcuts and toggle the button.

(go back to previous step if needed)


3. Fully allow untrusted shortcuts

A) Flip the "Allow untrusted shortcuts" toggle on.

B) Read the message and then confirm the dialog.

C) Enter your pin to confirm the settings.


4. Add untrusted shortcuts

A) Click on the shortcut link that was provided to you by the developer to attempt to add it. You should see something similar to this picture after clicking on the link.

B) Change the name of the command you want to use with Siri.

C) Look at each action in the shortcut to see what's happening.

C) Scroll all the way down to find the "Add untrusted shortcuts" button.


If you want more information about the shortcut, then contact the developer who sent it to you.

If the short looks good to you, then click on the "Add untrusted shortcut" button. After adding, the shortcut will appear in your collection.
